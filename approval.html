Was It Justified?

The Exmouth View Hotel sat just outside the Babbacombe Downs Conservation Area, meaning it lacked the architectural significance to warrant preservation. Originally two Victorian houses merged and expanded over time, its dated 1970s aesthetic contributed little to the area's charm. Many saw demolition and redevelopment as an improvement rather than a loss, given Babbacombe's mix of historic and modern buildings.

Supporters argued that replacing the aging hotel with 12 apartments and two townhouses would enhance the neighborhood while providing much-needed housing. Maintaining the hotel had become costly, and its viability as a business was declining. However, not everyone welcomed the change. Some residents lamented the loss of another traditional hotel, fearing it would alter the area's character and increase strain on local infrastructure. Others worried that reducing tourist accommodations could impact Babbacombe's appeal.

Despite objections, Torbay Council approved the redevelopment in 2019, marking the end of the Exmouth View Hotel and the beginning of a new chapter for the site.
